Population/New Infections Trends

The data used for the dashboard can be downloaded here for further analysis

The dashboard sought to explore the population of the countries that actively report HIV cases in Africa, the newly identified cases in 2009 and the cumulative incidence of HIV in the continent.

Findings from the exploration showed that as the population in the continent increased the number of new HIV cases identified reduced. This signifies that one or a combination of the following is happening: 1 - Improved health systems 2 - Improved HIV prevention and control 3 - A sign of epidemic control

Eventhough one or all of the above is happening in the continent, it important to know that active steps were taken to see the gains that are currently being witnessed. Sustainable measures should be put in place to ensure that the gains are sustained.
